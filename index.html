Do You Know? About Qatar

Qatar is a small, wealthy country located in the Persian Gulf region, known for its modern and innovative architecture, including the iconic Burj Qatar and the Museum of Islamic Art. It is also famous for its dedication to preserving its culture and heritage, which can be seen in attractions such as the Souq Waqif and the Katara Cultural Village. Additionally, Qatar is home to many international events, such as the FIFA World Cup 2022, which will be the first to be held in the Middle East. Often, it is referred to as the world’s richest country. The Emir of Qatar is HH Sheikh Tamim bin Hamad Al Thani.

  • Qatar is the world’s richest country in terms of GDP per capita, thanks to its vast oil and natural gas reserves.
  • Despite its wealth, Qatar has a relatively small population of around 2.8 million people, with only about 12% of them being Qatari citizens.
  • Qatar is the first Arab country to host the FIFA World Cup, which is scheduled to take place in 2022.
  • The country is home to the world’s tallest building with a clock tower, the iconic Abraj Al Bait Clock Tower in Mecca, Saudi Arabia.
  • Qatar is home to one of the world’s largest man-made islands, The Pearl-Qatar, which was built on a former pearl diving site and includes luxury residences, hotels, and shopping centers.
  • The country has one of the lowest crime rates in the world and is considered to be one of the safest places to live in the Middle East.
  • Qatar has one of the highest percentages of expatriates in the world, with foreign workers making up around 85% of the population.
  • The traditional sport of Qatar is falconry, and the country has a long history of breeding and training falcons for hunting.
  • The Qatari capital of Doha is home to several world-class museums and cultural institutions, including the Museum of Islamic Art, the National Museum of Qatar, and the Qatar National Library.
  • Qatar has made significant investments in renewable energy in recent years, including the development of one of the world’s largest solar power plants, the Al-Kharsaah Solar Power Plant, which is expected to be operational by 2022.

Qatar National's Things

    1. National Flag

The flag of Qatar features a maroon-colored background with a white band on the left side and nine serrated edges. The white band contains a vertical serrated stripe with nine white triangles.

    1. National Dress

|The traditional dress of Qatar is called the thobe or thawb, which is a long, loose-fitting garment worn by men. Women typically wear the abaya, a long black robe that covers the entire body.

    1. National Animal

The national animal of Qatar is the Arabian oryx, a species of antelope that is native to the Arabian Peninsula. It is also featured on the country’s coat of arms.
